# SESSION 1 FALLBACK: "AI or Human?" Game

**Duration:** 45 minutes
**Materials Needed:** Printed AI vs Human examples (see below), discussion cards

## Activity Structure

### Part 1: The Game (20 min)

**Instructions:**
1. Divide class into pairs or small groups
2. Distribute "AI or Human?" cards
3. For each example, groups decide: Was this written by AI or a human?
4. After each round, reveal answer and discuss

**Example Cards (Print and Cut):**

---
**CARD 1:**
"The mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell. It produces ATP through cellular respiration, converting glucose into energy that cells can use."

**ANSWER:** Could be either! This is a common phrase that appears in both human textbooks and AI responses. **Key point:** AI learns from human writing, so sometimes they're indistinguishable for simple facts.

---
**CARD 2:**
"I remember the exact moment I realized I wanted to be a doctor. I was eight years old, sitting in the hospital waiting room while my grandmother recovered from surgery. The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, and I watched the nurses move with quiet purpose..."

**ANSWER:** Human. Personal memories with sensory details (buzzing lights, grandmother) are human experiences AI cannot have. **Key point:** AI can write LIKE this, but can't have genuine memories.

---
**CARD 3:**
"Climate change is caused by several factors. First, the burning of fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide. Second, deforestation reduces the planet's ability to absorb CO2. Third, industrial processes emit greenhouse gases. In conclusion, addressing climate change requires a multi-faceted approach."

**ANSWER:** Likely AI. The rigid "First, Second, Third, In conclusion" structure and generic content are AI patterns. **Key point:** AI often writes in predictable structures.

---
**CARD 4:**
"The Battle of Gettysburg, fought from July 1-3, 1864, was a decisive Confederate victory that turned the tide of the American Civil War."

**ANSWER:** AI with errors! The date is wrong (1863) and outcome is inverted (Union victory). **Key point:** AI can sound confident while being completely wrong.

---
**CARD 5:**
"ngl this homework is lowkey impossible ğŸ˜­ been staring at it for like 2 hours and still dont get derivatives"

**ANSWER:** Human. Current slang, emoji use, and authentic frustration are human markers. **Key point:** AI tends toward formal language unless specifically prompted for casual.

---

### Part 2: Discussion (15 min)

**Discussion Questions:**
1. What patterns help you identify AI writing?
2. What makes human writing unique?
3. Why is it important to be able to tell the difference?
4. When is "AI or Human" NOT the right question? (Sometimes it doesn't matter; what matters is accuracy)

### Part 3: Create Your Own (10 min)

**Activity:**
Students write two short paragraphs on the same topicâ€”one imitating AI style, one in their authentic voice. Partners try to identify which is which.

- What would YOU add that AI couldn't?

# SESSION 8 FALLBACK: Charter Writing & Discussion

**Duration:** 45 minutes
**Materials Needed:** Charter templates, discussion cards

## Activity Structure

### Part 1: Draft Personal AI Use Charter (20 min)

**Charter Template:**

```
MY PERSONAL AI USE CHARTER

I, __________________, commit to using AI as a tool that
enhances my learning without replacing my thinking.

I WILL USE AI TO:
1. _______________________________________________________
2. _______________________________________________________
3. _______________________________________________________

I WILL NOT USE AI TO:
1. _______________________________________________________
2. _______________________________________________________
3. _______________________________________________________

I WILL MAINTAIN TRANSPARENCY BY:
1. _______________________________________________________
2. _______________________________________________________

THE HUMAN SKILLS I COMMIT TO DEVELOPING:
1. _______________________________________________________
2. _______________________________________________________
3. _______________________________________________________

Signed: ________________________ Date: ____________
```

### Part 2: Partner Share & Feedback (10 min)

Exchange charters with a partner:
- What commitment do you respect most?
- What might be hard to keep?
- Any suggestions?

### Part 3: Future of AI Discussion (15 min)

**Discussion Questions:**
1. What AI capabilities might exist in 5 years that don't exist now?
2. What human skills will ALWAYS matter, no matter how good AI gets?
3. What concerns you most about AI in society?
4. What excites you most about AI in your future?
5. What's one thing you'll do differently now that you've taken this course?
